You are a data analyst assessing how quickly different Census measurements change over time.

Classify the temporal stability of the following Census table. Temporal stability describes how rapidly the underlying phenomenon tends to change year over year in real life.

Stability levels:
- very_stable: Changes less than 1% per year on average (e.g., housing structure type, place of birth for established populations, geographic identifiers)
- stable: Changes 1-3% per year (e.g., educational attainment, language spoken at home, homeownership rates)
- moderate: Changes 3-10% per year (e.g., employment status, income levels, poverty rates, commuting patterns)
- volatile: Can change more than 10% per year (e.g., unemployment in specific subgroups during economic disruptions, recent migration flows, post-crisis indicators)
